### Early Basing

Early basing is never good. Not only does it delay the time you are able to gold, also the missed lumber opportunity from building towers from the start of the game is too punishing. If titan pearls it early he can buy 2 wards and 2 shields and siege it if it's in the first 2 minutes of the match. Also if titan gets a minion before you are able to get 45g for worker health upgrade he can park it outside your base and prevent you from lumbering, making you unable to scale and eventually the base will fall for free. It's an outdated strategy which used to be somewhat okay in the past, but in the current version it's not viable.

### Deciding When To Base

Once you have 45g you are fine to base if you have 1k lumber per elapsed minute of gametime. You should be double basing and of course having prepared food supply and RCs built before comitting. Preferably you want to base on the opposite side of the map from where the titan is to give you maximum time to set up the base.

It's possible to base without having 45g. In case of an early minion and a low -gs game, in the current version if there is a early minion builders must instantly create 4 double bases and deny all gold feed to have a chance to win. You could try to kill the minion but it's unlikely to succeed vs a decent titan as the minion will have ankh. 

### Blinkproofing

Titan has multiple ways to jump into our base with mirror, ankh, burrow, volt ult. Because of this if we want to play optimal we should always be blinkproofing our bases.

### Hunters in base

Having a hunter in your base can be great if managed properly. The problem with hunters in bases are titan will not start sieging until the hunter is nuked out and dead or it will chain the nuke deep in your base so you die. Flying and 1x1 hunters are exempt of course because you can withdraw them if titan starts nuking. You could think it's fine to have an empty slot in the tower area and a large empty area in the back where we blink back our hunter if low health, this is bad because we lose too much damage from not towering the base fully and the base will not be blinkproofed leaving it susceptible to be jumped. ### Workerblock

Worker block is when you build shelters in strategic positions in your base such that they will spawn workers on the ramp while titan is sieging. The effect is twofold, it hinders titan from entering your base and leaving your base forcing him to comitt to a siege and not just attack once and back off to regen to full. Check the baselayouts on the discord for the exact shelter placements. It's important if you are doublebasing with someone, whoever is controlling the workerblock should not have shared controls of your partner. This is because when you box select your workers you could accidentally include your partners builder in the selection and issue commands you don't intend. 

Worker block feeds a shittone, don't do it unless necessary. Absolutely don't workerblock if you don't have worker health upgrade, it feeds too much and barely blocks, the towers you gain from the shelters will serve you better.

### Towering effectively

When you are upgrading your towers from supers to megas most towers get a substantial range increase, should we upgrade our front towers or back towers to megas first? Either choice is viable, I like to keep my megas in the front if titan has a lot of minions to focus fire them if titan tries to nuke my rewall. The other option will increase your base dps during the siege, which is also nice of course if you have a tanky rewaller.

Ensure your towers keep hitting in the most likely siege path the titan will take, you want to avoid titan taking a turn once inside your base and your towers become out of range. Also if you are vs glacious or noxious consider their unique disables towers in an aoe, build towers such that they can never disable too many of your towers.

If you are double basing with another builder whoever has lowest range towers should tower in the front. 

### Economy of food

A normal builder usually has a lumber income of 1000 lumber per minute, this is a bit higher or lower depending on the distance to return lumber and which tier of lumber harvest acquisition rate you currently have researched. But for simplicity let's assume you have 1k per minute. With 6 food per worker you have 25 workers, 1000/25=40 lumber per minute per worker. Building towers in batches of 6 will therefore have an opportunity cost of 40 lumber per minute, or 800 lumber over a 20 minute period. This means ideally we want to build as few towers as possible while still having our base sufficient damage protecting us from being sieged, i.e. upgrade all towers to megas before building another batch of 6. Unfortunately this is a dream scenario. Investing all lumber into megas and having empty lumber spots will be drastically less damage than filling your base with supers. You need to find the sweet spot between full megas and full supers for each game which depends on how fed titan is and how much lumber pooling you expect to get.

### Lumberpooling

Lumberpooling is when one builder buys items for another builder to sell, it transfers 50% of the lumber. Only builders and hunters are able to sell items, you can only sell to merchants and to the secret shop. If you are double basing with someone and you're not contributing towers you will have lumber to spare which you should pool to your partner. 

If you have had an unfortunate game where your lumber income is far less than 1000 lumber per minute, consider pooling your lumber to another builder to increase the builders team chance to win.
